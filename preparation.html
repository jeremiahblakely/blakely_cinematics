SESSION PREPARATION GUIDE

Everything you need to know to prepare for your cinematic portrait session and achieve stunning results

1

Before Your Session

1-2 Weeks Prior

Schedule Your Preparation

Great portraits start with great preparation. Here's your pre-session checklist:

  • Book your hair appointment 2-3 days before the session (not the day of)
  • Schedule any grooming appointments (eyebrows, facial hair trim) 2-3 days prior
  • If hiring a makeup artist, book them at least 2 weeks in advance
  • Start planning your wardrobe selections
  • Get plenty of rest the week of your session
  • Stay hydrated - it shows in your skin!

Pro Tip

Avoid trying new skincare products or treatments within a week of your session. Stick with your regular routine to avoid unexpected reactions.

Wardrobe Selection

Choosing the Right Outfits

For Theatrical & Commercial Headshots

For Creative/Cinematic Portraits

More freedom to express your personality and brand:

  • Textures and layers add visual interest

Important Note

Bring more options than you think you'll need. It's better to have choices during the session. All clothing should be clean, pressed, and on hangers.

3

Hair & Makeup

Camera-Ready Appearance

Professional Makeup (Recommended)

We recommend hiring a professional makeup artist familiar with photography makeup. Camera-ready makeup is different from everyday makeup - it needs to withstand lights and translate well on camera.

  • Book your makeup artist through our Recommended Vendors page
  • Allow 60-90 minutes for professional makeup
  • Bring reference photos of looks you like
  • Communicate any skin sensitivities

DIY Makeup Tips

If doing your own makeup, remember these camera-specific tips:

  • Apply slightly heavier than daily wear (camera lights wash out makeup)
  • Use matte products to minimize shine
  • Define your eyes and brows - they're focal points
  • Avoid SPF products (they can cause flashback)
  • Bring powder, lipstick, and concealer for touch-ups
  • Keep makeup consistent with your usual audition look

Hair Preparation

  • Style your hair as you would for auditions
  • Avoid drastic cuts or colors right before the session
  • Bring styling tools for touch-ups between looks
  • Consider multiple styles if time permits

Day of Your Session

Session Day Timeline

Your Session Day Schedule

Night Before
Get 8+ hours of sleep, avoid alcohol, prepare outfits
Morning Of
Light breakfast, hydrate, avoid salty foods
2 Hours Before
Do makeup/hair or meet your makeup artist
30 Minutes Before
Final touch-ups, get into first outfit
Arrival
Arrive on time, relaxed, and ready to create!

What to Bring

  • All wardrobe options (on hangers)
  • Makeup for touch-ups
  • Hair styling tools and products
  • Water and light snacks
  • Any props relevant to your brand
  • Positive energy and openness to direction!

Remember

The more relaxed and prepared you are, the better your photos will be. Trust the process and enjoy the experience!
